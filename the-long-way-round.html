Driving in the UK

A few things worth knowing before you pick up the keys at Stansted.

Stay left

Traffic keeps to the left and roundabouts run clockwise — give way to anyone already on the roundabout, coming from your right.

Mind the mph

Limits are in miles per hour: 70 on motorways, 60 on most single-carriageway A-roads, and as low as 20 through town centres. Average-speed cameras are common on the A1, so cruise at the limit rather than the flow of traffic.

Check the fuel cap

Confirm petrol or diesel at the rental desk — both pumps sit side by side at every station and the cars look identical.

London's charges

If you park near King's Cross on the Friday, check whether the spot sits inside the Congestion Charge zone — it's £18 on weekdays, 7am to 6pm. Almost all modern rental cars meet the ULEZ emissions standard, so that charge shouldn't apply, but it's worth confirming on the TfL site beforehand.

Park on the edge, walk the middle

Cambridge, York and Edinburgh all have narrow medieval centres with restricted or pedestrianised streets. Use a car park on the edge of town and cover the old town on foot.

Passing places aren't parking spaces

The roads around Glencoe, Glenfinnan and Loch Lomond narrow to a single track in places. Pull into a passing place on your left for oncoming traffic, or wait opposite one if it's on the right — and never park in one. A wave to say thanks is standard.

Fuel up before the glens

Petrol stations thin out fast once you're off the main road. Top up in Glencoe or Fort William before the Glenfinnan loop, and again before the long final push south.

Pack for midges

Highland midges come out in force on calm summer evenings near water — expect them at Loch Shiel, Ballachulish and the Loch Lomond shore. A repellent earns its space in the car.
